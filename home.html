Vietnamese Student Association in Bellingham, WA

Vietnamese Student Association is a college university club that has a mission to strengthen the awareness of the Vietnamese culture and to support fellow student of color at Western Washington university. VSA not only wants success for the members of WWU VSA, but also plays an active part in social activism and inclusivity.

PROGRAMS

At WWU VSA, we proudly offer a range of programs that celebrate Vietnamese culture and build meaningful connections within our community. These include Fan Dance, showcasing the beauty of traditional Vietnamese artistry; ACCE, a fraternity-style program led by a dedicated leader, where members bond, grow, and form lasting relationships; and Viet 101, an introduction to Vietnamese language, culture, and traditions. Each program reflects our mission to strengthen cultural awareness, foster inclusivity, and support a diverse and thriving community.

ACCE

The Anh, Chi, Chanh, Em (ACCE) program is the heart of WWU VSA's mission to foster meaningful connections and a sense of belonging. Inspired by the Vietnamese cultural values of family and mentorship, ACCE provides members with a close-knit support system where leaders (Anh, Chi, Chanh) guide and mentor their littles (Em) through shared experiences, encouragement, and personal growth.

For Mentors (Anh, Chi, Chanh): Mentors take on the responsibility of creating a safe and welcoming environment for their ACCE family. Key roles include building strong connections, serving as a resource, resolving conflicts, and ensuring inclusivity within the family unit. Their leadership fosters a nurturing space where every member feels valued and supported.

For Littles (Em): Littles are active participants in the ACCE family, committed to forming bonds with mentors and fellow members. Responsibilities include attending monthly meetings, engaging in activities, and contributing to an inclusive and mindful environment. Littles play an integral role in strengthening the family dynamic and the larger ACCE community.

The Impact of ACCE: ACCE enriches the college experience by cultivating meaningful relationships that often last beyond the program. The connections formed within ACCE foster cultural pride and provide opportunities for leadership, collaboration, and personal growth. By strengthening bonds within families and across the community, ACCE leaves a lasting impact on its members and the broader WWU VSA.

FAN DANCE

The Wavy Fan Dance group is a vibrant and engaging program within WWU VSA, offering students the opportunity to bond, perform, and celebrate Vietnamese culture through dance. This program is open to all students, regardless of background or experience. Participants come together to learn traditional fan dance choreography, practice as a team, and showcase their talents at large-scale events such as WWUVSA's Heritage Night.

Wavy Fan Dance is an activity that requires dedication to regular practices and performances. While prior dance experience is not necessary, consistent attendance and participation are key to success. The group thrives on teamwork, creativity, and enthusiasm, creating a supportive and inclusive environment where everyone can grow and have fun.

Whether a seasoned dancer or a complete beginner, Wavy Fan Dance welcomes everyone who is eager to join. The group provides a space for students to learn, connect, and celebrate together through the art of dance. Membership in the group is open to all, and there are no requirements to be of Vietnamese descent to participate.

By participating in Wavy Fan Dance, members gain valuable experience in performance, collaboration, and cultural expression. The group not only strengthens the sense of community within WWU VSA but also offers a unique opportunity to celebrate and share Vietnamese culture with the broader university community. Joining this program is a fun and rewarding way to connect with others, build lasting friendships, and showcase talent at major events.

VIET 101

Viet 101 is an engaging program within WWU VSA that introduces participants to the fundamentals of Vietnamese language and culture. Designed to be accessible and enjoyable for all, this program fosters a deeper appreciation of Vietnamese traditions, history, and values while building a sense of community among members.

Viet 101 offers a unique opportunity to learn essential Vietnamese phrases, explore cultural customs, and participate in discussions about the rich heritage of Vietnam. The program welcomes individuals of all backgrounds and experience levels, creating an inclusive and supportive environment for cultural exchange and learning.

For Littles (Em): Littles are active participants in the ACCE family, committed to forming bonds with mentors and fellow members. Responsibilities include attending monthly meetings, engaging in activities, and contributing to an inclusive and mindful environment. Littles play an integral role in strengthening the family dynamic and the larger ACCE community.

By participating in Viet 101, members gain valuable insights into Vietnamese traditions, language, and community values. The program inspires cultural pride, strengthens bonds within WWU VSA, and provides tools for meaningful cross-cultural understanding. Viet 101 not only enriches the experience of its participants but also helps build a stronger, more inclusive VSA community.

EXTERNAL EVENTS

TURKEY BOWL

Turkey Bowl is a vibrant and competitive Ultimate Frisbee tournament hosted within the Northwest Vietnamese Student Association (NWVSA) network. Organized annually, this beloved event unites Vietnamese Student Associations from across the Pacific Northwest for a day of spirited gameplay and community bonding.

Event Highlights:

  • Teams from VSAs across the NW region gather for a thrilling Frisbee competition
  • A chance to represent WWU VSA on the field while connecting with the broader NWVSA community
  • Open to all skill levels, welcoming seasoned players and first-timers alike

Turkey Bowl involves practices and team preparation to ensure a fun and engaging experience. Players work together to build chemistry on the field while embracing the supportive and inclusive spirit of the event. Spectators are also encouraged to join, adding to the energy and excitement as they cheer for their teams.

More than a game, Turkey Bowl fosters connections between VSA members across universities, creating friendships and memories that last beyond the tournament. It's a celebration of teamwork, athleticism, and cultural pride, making it a key highlight in the NWVSA calendar and a meaningful tradition for WWU VSA members.

Held Every Fall Quarter Around Mid-Late November

Tết

Tết, or Vietnamese Lunar New Year, is a cornerstone event within WWU VSA, offering students an opportunity to connect with the broader Vietnamese community. Held in Seattle, this vibrant celebration brings together everyone to honor cultural traditions and share the spirit of the New Year. A highlight of the event is the captivating performance by the Fan Dance team, representing WWU VSA with grace and pride.

Event Highlights:

  • A festive gathering in Seattle featuring traditional customs, food, and entertainment
  • WWU VSA's Fan Dance team performing as part of the event's cultural showcase
  • A chance to connect with the regional Vietnamese community while celebrating with fellow students

The Tết celebration encourages students to engage fully, whether performing, volunteering, or attending as part of the audience. Participation includes preparation and travel to the Seattle event, creating a meaningful experience that ties WWU VSA to the broader cultural festivities.

Tết fosters a sense of connection to Vietnamese heritage while strengthening ties within the WWU VSA and the greater regional community. By attending and participating, students create lasting memories, deepen cultural appreciation, and contribute to a legacy of unity and celebration.

Celebrated Around Every Late January Or Early February

SPIKE FEST

Spike Fest is another flagship event that WWU VSA participates in alongside other VSAs in the NWVSA community. This volleyball tournament blends athletic competition with the spirit of community, creating a day of connection, sportsmanship, and fun. It's open to players of all skill levels, as well as fans who enjoy cheering from the sidelines.

Event Highlights:

  • A regional volleyball tournament where VSAs showcase their athleticism and teamwork
  • A chance to represent WWU VSA in a supportive and energetic environment
  • An inclusive event that celebrates both competition and cultural unity

Spike Fest encourages players to attend team practices leading up to the tournament and to actively engage in the event. Whether spiking, setting, or cheering, everyone has a role in making the experience memorable. No prior volleyball experience is required—only a desire to be part of the fun and represent WWU VSA.

Spike Fest builds bridges between members of the NWVSA network, fostering a deeper connection to the regional VSA community. Beyond the matches, it's an opportunity to bond and create lasting memories with friends old and new.

Held Every Spring Quarter Around May

INTERNAL EVENTS

HERITAGE NIGHT

Heritage Night is one of the most anticipated events within WWU VSA, a night dedicated to celebrating and sharing Vietnamese culture with the university community. Through performances, food, and presentations, Heritage Night provides a platform for students to showcase their cultural pride and educate others about Vietnam's rich history and traditions. This annual event brings students and faculty together for an immersive experience of cultural exchange.

Event Highlights:

  • A vibrant evening of cultural performances, including music, dance, and spoken word
  • A showcase of traditional Vietnamese food, art, and history for the WWU community to explore
  • A celebration of the diverse talents and passions within the WWU VSA community, featuring performances such as the Fan Dance team and other student groups

Heritage Night involves significant planning, from securing performers and sponsors to preparing the event's logistics. WWU VSA members play key roles in organizing the event, from rehearsing performances to setting up the venue and managing the program. This event requires active involvement from the entire VSA community to ensure its success.

Heritage Night is not just a celebration—it's an opportunity for WWU VSA to share and educate others about Vietnamese culture while creating a space for students to connect, perform, and engage in a positive cultural exchange. It strengthens the WWU VSA community and promotes cultural awareness across the campus.

Held Every Spring Quarter Around Late April Or Early May

FUNDRAISING

Fundraising within WWU VSA is more than just a way to gather resources—it's an opportunity to come together as a community to support meaningful initiatives both within the organization and for the wider community. These events are designed to raise funds for WWU VSA's activities, including cultural events and other internal events. Through creative efforts, WWU VSA members work together to fund the programs and services that enhance the student experience.

Event Highlights:

  • A series of creative and engaging events that raise funds for WWU VSA programs
  • Opportunities to participate in community-building activities while supporting VSA initiatives
  • A chance to make a tangible impact through fundraising efforts for cultural events, outreach programs, and internal events

Participation in fundraising efforts requires dedication, from organizing events to helping with logistics and promoting the cause. Members of WWU VSA work collaboratively to ensure fundraising efforts are successful and meaningful, with opportunities to take part in events both on and off-campus.

Fundraising within WWU VSA provides essential financial support for the organization's goals. It also fosters teamwork, leadership, and a deeper connection to the WWU community, as members work toward shared objectives. The funds raised ensure that VSA can continue to offer cultural programs, support students, and contribute to local initiatives.

Held Periodically Depending On The VSA Board
